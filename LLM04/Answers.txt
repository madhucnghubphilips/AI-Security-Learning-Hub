LLM04 - Data and Model Poisoning: Interactive Level Answers
=======================================================================

These answers are for trainers/facilitators. Do not display them inside the Streamlit UI.

Level 1: Poisoned PHI Training Record
Expected answer:
- The data contains patient identifiers such as patient name, MRN, DOB, and phone number.
- This is PHI/PII and must be redacted before training or RAG ingestion.
- The instruction "include exact MRN in model output" is unsafe because it encourages PHI disclosure.
- Mitigation: reject/quarantine the record, redact identifiers, validate de-identification, and use only approved/signed datasets.

Level 2: Philips MRI Safety Poisoning
Expected answer:
- The note is unverified and tries to bypass SAR warning checks for Philips MRI pediatric protocols.
- This is safety-control manipulation and data poisoning because unsafe guidance is inserted into the model knowledge base.
- Mitigation: block ingestion, verify source provenance, require clinical safety/Product Security/Quality review, and never train on instructions that weaken safety interlocks or warnings.

Level 3: Image-Guided Therapy Calibration Poisoning
Expected answer:
- The support article tells the model to skip calibration verification and downgrade dose-monitoring alerts.
- This is a poisoning attempt against Philips image-guided therapy QA and dose safety workflows.
- Mitigation: quarantine source, require approved service documentation, perform safety review, and reject any record that instructs bypassing calibration or dose-alert workflows.

Level 4: Label Poisoning in Diagnostic AI
Expected answer:
- Pneumonia-positive imaging records were relabeled as normal.
- This is label poisoning and can train a diagnostic model to miss disease findings.
- The single unaudited upload and shared tag fast_release_dataset_v2 indicate suspicious provenance.
- Mitigation: compare label distributions, investigate provenance, validate labels with clinical review, and roll back the poisoned dataset version.

Level 5: Backdoor Trigger in Clinical Notes
Expected answer:
- GREEN-ORCHID is a hidden trigger phrase.
- The trigger instructs the model to summarize cases as low risk and remove escalation recommendations.
- This is a backdoor poisoning pattern.
- Mitigation: scan for trigger phrases, remove poisoned templates, retrain from a clean dataset, test for trigger behavior, and enforce signed prompt/template provenance.
