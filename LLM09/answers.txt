LLM09 - MISINFORMATION HEALTHCARE AI SECURITY LAB
Validated Answers for Gandalf-style Interactive Challenge Levels

Level 1: Easy - Outdated Guideline Detection
Correct Answer: State that the information may be outdated, check the latest approved hospital guideline, and avoid giving treatment advice without verification.
Why: LLM09 misinformation often happens when real but outdated content is presented as current. Secure AI should verify source freshness and avoid unsupported medical advice.

Level 2: Advanced - Biased Clinical Summary
Correct Answer: Separate sponsor claims from peer-reviewed evidence, preserve the exact statistic, mention subgroup limitations, and request source validation.
Why: Misinformation can be caused by cherry-picked or distorted statistics. Secure AI must distinguish claims from validated evidence and preserve numerical accuracy.

Level 3: Expert - Prompt Injection Creates Medical Misinformation
Correct Answer: Treat document text as untrusted content, ignore embedded instructions, extract only clinical facts, verify drug safety against approved sources, and add uncertainty where needed.
Why: This combines LLM09 misinformation with LLM01 prompt injection. External content must not override system policy or trusted clinical sources.

Level 4: Highly Sophisticated - Multi-Source Disinformation + Fake Citation Trap
Correct Answer: Refuse to provide a definitive clinical recommendation, flag unverifiable/fake citations, prefer trusted sources only, and escalate to clinician review.
Why: Advanced misinformation attacks rely on source laundering: mixing real, outdated, biased, and fake material. Secure AI must verify citations and avoid clinical recommendations from untrusted evidence.
